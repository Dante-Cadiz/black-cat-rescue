Why black cats?

  • Black cats are often the last cats adopted from shelters.
  • On average, it takes 10 days longer for a black or black and white cat to be adopted than for a ginger one.
  • This partly stems from historical superstition about black cats being unlucky.
  • According to researchers, the advent of social media has also been influential, as black cats are perceived as less photogenic.
  • Despite negative perceptions about black cats, they have been shown to be some of the most intelligent and loving animals.
  • Your home will surely be improved by the presence of a black cat.

Why rescue?

  • The current cat population in shelters in the UK is 3.2 million.
  • This is comprised of both kittens with feral parents and adult cats who have been abandoned.
  • Many of the second category were once 'lockdown cats' bought by irresponsible owners and then given up on.
  • Adopting a rescue cat from a shelter gives these beautiful creatures a second chance at happiness.
  • By adopting you are helping TWO animals - the one you adopted and the one for whom a shelter place has been freed up.
  • Rescue animals are extremely loving and affectionate - they know you have saved them.
